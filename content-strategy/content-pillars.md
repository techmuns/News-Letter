# Munshot — Long-Term Content Pillars

> **The strategic bet.** Munshot doesn't win attention by talking about Munshot. It wins
> by becoming the place institutional investors go to think more clearly. The product is
> AI agents that automate institutional research; the content is the *proof* of that
> worldview — rigorous, data-led, framework-first intelligence that a fund manager would
> forward to a colleague. Promotion is a by-product of being useful, never the point.

These eight pillars are **evergreen**. They are not a campaign calendar — they are the
permanent lanes the brand publishes in. News gives us pegs; the pillars give us a spine.
Every idea, newsletter, and article in this repo maps to exactly one pillar, so coverage
stays balanced and the brand's point of view compounds instead of scattering.

**Audience across all pillars:** portfolio managers, hedge-fund analysts, PE and VC
investors, buy-side and sell-side analysts, allocators, and the research leaders who run
them. Global frameworks, with select India/EM angles (DRHPs, promoter governance) where
they sharpen the point.

## 01 · Research Tradecraft
**Mandate:** Show how great research is *made* — the process, not just the conclusion.

**Why it earns trust.** Every buy-side reader has a research process they're insecure
about. Content that names the craft — variant perception, disconfirmation, base rates,
the audit trail — signals that Munshot understands their job at the level of a peer, not a
vendor. This is the pillar where "AI that automates research" is most credible, because we
demonstrate we know what good research even is.

**Core sub-themes:** variant perception · the three edges (informational / analytical /
behavioral) · designing a repeatable pipeline · expected-value-of-information · primary
research and expert networks · outside view and base rates · making the narrative testable
· the one-page thesis · the capital cycle as a filter · institutional memory.

**Signature formats:** "How we'd research X in 30 minutes" teardown · annotated thesis
memos · the disconfirmation checklist · "what consensus is missing" variant-perception notes.

**How Munshot shows up (quietly).** The last line points to the agent that *runs* the
pipeline — surfacing disconfirming evidence, keeping the audit trail, remembering what the
desk already learned. The pointer is a footnote to a genuinely useful piece.

**Flagship piece:** *"The only edge that compounds: a working guide to variant perception."*

## 02 · Market Science
**Mandate:** Treat markets as a science — with hypotheses, evidence, and error bars.

**Why it earns trust.** Quant-literate readers are allergic to hand-waving. A pillar that
takes factor decay, overfitting, and multiple-comparisons seriously earns respect from the
most skeptical, highest-value segment of the audience — and it directly showcases the
analytical muscle behind an AI research platform.

**Core sub-themes:** factor premia · where expected returns come from · alpha decay and
crowding · backtest overfitting · survivorship and look-ahead bias · signal vs noise ·
p-hacking · alternative data · causality vs correlation · regime change · the fundamental
law of active management.

**Signature formats:** "Signal or noise?" data autopsies · factor explainers · "seven ways
a backtest lies to you" · reproducing a famous study and stress-testing it.

**How Munshot shows up.** The pointer is to disciplined signal-testing — the platform that
checks whether an "edge" survives out-of-sample before it costs you.

**Flagship piece:** *"Every edge is a melting ice cube: a field guide to alpha decay."*

## 03 · Financial Forensics
**Mandate:** Read the filing the way a short-seller reads it — for what's hiding.

**Why it earns trust.** This is the most *immediately useful* pillar. A reader who learns
one new red flag they can apply to their next 10-K got real value in five minutes. It's
also where Munshot's document-intelligence story (DRHP diffing, filing analysis) is most
natural without ever being a pitch.

**Core sub-themes:** quality of earnings · cash conversion · accounting shenanigans ·
revenue-recognition red flags · footnote reading · working-capital early warnings · cohort
and unit economics · reverse-engineering implied expectations · ROIC vs WACC · intangibles
and write-downs · sum-of-the-parts · classic fraud case studies.

**Signature formats:** "Red flag of the week" · annotated 10-K / DRHP walkthroughs · "the
number that isn't in the headline" · forensic post-mortems of blow-ups.

**How Munshot shows up.** The pointer is to the tool that diffs successive filings and
surfaces the amendment before the listing — exactly the "risk hides in the edit" thesis
already seeded in the app's mock content.

**Flagship piece:** *"Cash is a fact, earnings an opinion: seven shenanigans in any 10-K."*

## 04 · Decision Frameworks
**Mandate:** Give the reader a reusable tool they'll screenshot and keep.

**Why it earns trust.** Frameworks are the most *saved and shared* content on LinkedIn for
this audience. A crisp checklist that survives contact with a real decision builds
authority faster than any essay. This pillar is the brand's engagement flywheel.

**Core sub-themes:** pre-mortems · inversion · second-order thinking · expected value over
being right · reference-class forecasting · latticework of mental models · kill criteria ·
margin of safety as a rule · circle of competence · opportunity cost · Bayesian updating ·
model risk · ergodicity.

**Signature formats:** one-graphic frameworks · "steal this checklist" carousels · the
pre-mortem template · decision-rule cards.

**How Munshot shows up.** The pointer positions the platform as where the framework gets
*operationalized* — run on every name in the book, not just remembered.

**Flagship piece:** *"Kill criteria: decide what breaks the thesis before you buy it."*

## 05 · The Behavioral Edge
**Mandate:** Name the cognitive traps that quietly cost basis points.

**Why it earns trust.** Every investor believes bias afflicts *other* people. Content that
holds up the mirror with specificity — the disposition effect, noise, escalation of
commitment — is both humbling and useful, and it travels because readers tag colleagues.
It also frames the case for a disciplined, agentic process without saying so.

**Core sub-themes:** loss aversion and the disposition effect · overconfidence and
calibration · anchoring · confirmation bias and self-justification · noise · the narrative
fallacy · recency and availability · herding and reflexivity · decision journals ·
incentive-caused bias · sunk cost · temperament as edge.

**Signature formats:** "the bias that cost you X" · decision-journal templates · "process
vs outcome" scorecards · calibration self-tests.

**How Munshot shows up.** The pointer frames agents as the antidote to human noise — a
consistent second reader that doesn't get anchored or tired.

**Flagship piece:** *"Noise: why the same analyst reaches two different conclusions."*

## 06 · Governance & Incentives
**Mandate:** Follow the incentives; the strategy and the risk are downstream of them.

**Why it earns trust.** Governance is where careful investors find asymmetric, under-priced
risk — and where lazy investors get blindsided. Rigorous governance content signals that
Munshot reads the proxy, not just the pitch deck. Highly relevant to PE/VC diligence and to
India/EM readers watching promoter behavior.

**Core sub-themes:** capital allocation as the CEO's real job · reading the proxy ·
related-party transactions · executive comp alignment · promoter pledging · auditor changes
· board quality · insider transactions · capital-allocation scorecards · serial acquirers ·
ESG signal vs noise · founder-mode vs steward-mode.

**Signature formats:** "governance smoke alarms" checklist · capital-allocation scorecards ·
proxy teardowns · "the footnote that mattered" case studies.

**How Munshot shows up.** The pointer is to structured diligence — turning a filing into a
governance workspace that flags related-party and comp red flags automatically.

**Flagship piece:** *"Capital allocation is the CEO's real job — here's the scorecard."*

## 07 · Portfolio, Risk & Market Structure
**Mandate:** The math of building a book and the plumbing it trades through.

**Why it earns trust.** Alpha gets the headlines; sizing, correlation, and liquidity decide
who survives. This pillar speaks to PMs and CIOs at the level of the decisions that actually
move fund returns, and it demonstrates quantitative seriousness without slipping into
academic abstraction.

**Core sub-themes:** risk as permanent loss vs volatility · Kelly and bet sizing ·
correlation regimes · drawdown/recovery asymmetry · tail hedging and convexity · fat tails ·
liquidity risk · diversification's limits · factor-exposure hygiene · stress testing ·
passive flows and the index effect · market microstructure · options positioning and dealer
gamma.

**Signature formats:** "the math they don't teach you" · drawdown/recovery tables ·
correlation-regime charts · "how your order actually trades" microstructure explainers.

**How Munshot shows up.** The pointer frames the platform as the always-on risk lens —
decomposing the book's real exposures faster than a quarterly review.

**Flagship piece:** *"Correlation goes to 1 exactly when you need it not to."*

## 08 · The AI-Native Research Firm
**Mandate:** Define the future of the research function — and let Munshot own the category.

**Why it earns trust.** This is the only pillar where Munshot is overtly the subject, so it
must be the *least* promotional and the most intellectually honest — including where AI
fails, where judgment is irreplaceable, and where model risk bites. Done right, it makes
Munshot the thought leader of a category it's helping create.

**Core sub-themes:** human + agent operating models · cheap prediction, valuable judgment ·
data as a moat · co-intelligence (model as teammate, not oracle) · model risk and
hallucination governance · the alignment problem for investors · institutional memory ·
strategic inflection points · knowledge compounding · radical uncertainty · research
provenance and auditability.

**Signature formats:** "the analyst of 2030" · human-vs-agent workflow teardowns ·
"where AI shouldn't be trusted yet" · provenance and audit-trail explainers.

**How Munshot shows up.** Here the pointer can be direct — but it lands *after* an argument
that would stand on its own even if Munshot didn't exist. Trust first, product second.

**Flagship piece:** *"When prediction gets cheap, judgment gets expensive."*

## How the pillars operate

- **One pillar per asset.** Tagging every piece keeps coverage balanced and makes the
  content calendar auditable at a glance.
- **Pillars → series → ideas.** Each pillar spawns recurring *series* (e.g. "Red flag of
  the week"), and each series spawns individual *ideas* — see
  [`100-linkedin-ideas.md`](./100-linkedin-ideas.md).
- **The 90/10 rule.** Across every pillar, insight-to-promotion holds at roughly 90/10.
  The product pointer is a quiet footnote to a piece that already earned its place. This is
  a configurable dial in the engine — see [`content-filters.md`](./content-filters.md).
- **Books as credibility anchors.** Each idea cites a real, canonical book. It signals we
  stand on the shoulders of the field's best thinking and gives the reader a next step —
  the most generous thing a "trusted source" can do.
